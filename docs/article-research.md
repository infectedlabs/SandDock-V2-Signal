# Sanddock Content Strategy: Keyword, GEO/AEO, Competitor Gap & Format Research (June 2026)

## TL;DR
- **Build a three-layer content engine**: (1) educational pillar/definition pages (Heikin Ashi, stop-loss/take-profit, "are crypto signals legit") that win informational search and LLM citations; (2) competitor comparison/alternative pages ("3Commas alternative," "Cryptohopper review") that capture high-intent commercial traffic; and (3) a transparency-led "track record" hub that is Sanddock's single biggest differentiator and the strongest trust signal for both Google E-E-A-T and LLM citation.
- **GEO/AEO is now a distinct discipline from SEO**: Averi's March 2026 analysis of 680 million AI citations found only 11% of domains are cited by both ChatGPT and Perplexity (independently corroborated by Whitehat SEO's study of 118,000 AI responses, which found "only 11% of cited domains appeared across multiple platforms," and by Passionfruit at 12% across three engines). The highest-leverage 2026 moves are answer-first 40-60 word opening blocks under question-style H2s, FAQPage + Article + Organization + SoftwareApplication schema, cited statistics, current-year ("2026") freshness signals, and off-site consensus (Reddit, G2, YouTube). Perplexity favors freshness/Reddit; ChatGPT favors authoritative brands; Google AI Overviews lean on pages already ranking.
- **The biggest competitor content gap is transparency and honest, beginner-first answers**: rivals (3Commas, Cornix, Cryptohopper) generate heavy complaint volume around hidden fees, auto-renewal/cancellation, and deleted losing trades. Sanddock's public immutable track record and anti-hype voice let it own queries competitors structurally cannot answer credibly ("is X legit," "X hidden fees," "are crypto signals worth it").

### 2. LLM citation optimization (GEO/AEO) - current as of 2026

**The core shift:** GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) earns citations *inside* AI answers; SEO earns ranked links. As noted above, the overlap between the two is now small (~11-12% of cited domains appear across multiple AI engines). So Sanddock must optimize for citation, not just ranking.

**What demonstrably correlates with citation (2026):**
- **Answer-first blocks.** Lead every section with a direct 40-60 word answer, then expand. Retrieval engines (Perplexity, ChatGPT search, AI Overviews) weight the opening passage most heavily - the first ~200 words carry disproportionate weight, and ~44.2% of LLM citations are extracted from the first 30% of a page (Wix/Evertune 2026).
- **Statistics + citations.** Aggarwal et al., "GEO: Generative Engine Optimization" (Princeton/Georgia Tech/IIT Delhi/Allen Institute, KDD 2024 - 9 tactics tested across 10,000 queries) found that adding Statistics lifted AI visibility +41% and adding direct Quotations from named sources +28%, while keyword stuffing *lowered* visibility.
- **Question-style H2/H3 headings** that mirror how users phrase queries, paired immediately with the answer.
- **Ranked listicles** ("Best X," "Top N," numbered): ~63% of LLM citations point to listicle pages per Evertune (May 2026), and ranked Top-N lists dominate commercial-query citations.
- **Freshness.** Perplexity favors content under 12 months old; it cited content published in the last 30 days at an 82% rate in one 2026 analysis, and visible "2026" year signals in titles/headings improve citation rates ~30%. Critical for crypto/YMYL.
- **Length is NOT the lever.** Ahrefs' study of 174,048 pages found a ~0.04 (essentially zero) correlation between word count and AI Overview citation; 53.4% of cited pages were under 1,000 words. Structure and passage density beat length.

**Schema markup - the honest verdict:** Schema does not *directly* get you cited by LLMs (LLMs tokenize JSON-LD and lose its structure; the visible Q&A text is what gets extracted), but it removes friction and is confirmed to help on Google/Microsoft pipelines. Microsoft's Fabrice Canel confirmed at SMX Munich (March 2025) that "schema markup helps Microsoft's LLMs understand content," and Google's Ryan Levering said the same. ~71% of pages cited by ChatGPT include structured data (SEranking). One study found pages with FAQPage schema achieved a 41% citation rate vs 15% without. **The five schema types that matter: Organization, Article/BlogPosting, FAQPage, HowTo, and (for the product) SoftwareApplication.** Note: Google restricted FAQ *rich results* to government/health sites in 2023, so FAQ schema's value now is AI/LLM comprehension, not SERP rich snippets. Use 1-2 schema types per page and only mark up visible content.

**Platform-specific selection logic:**
- **Perplexity** - real-time retrieval every query, no knowledge cutoff; can cite within hours; heavily weights freshness and Reddit (Reddit ≈ 24-46.5% of Perplexity citations across analyses); averages ~21.87 citations per response.
- **ChatGPT search** - hybrid of training data + Bing-powered retrieval; favors recognized/authoritative brands; cites brands in only ~0.59% of responses (vs Perplexity ~13%); 90% of its citations come from outside Google's top 20.
- **Google AI Overviews** - strongest correlation with existing organic rankings, but the share of citations from top-10 organic results dropped from ~76% (mid-2025) toward 17-38% (early 2026); Reddit, YouTube, Wikipedia heavily cited.
- **Claude/Gemini** - reward comprehensive, well-sourced content with clear citations.

**E-E-A-T for YMYL financial content:** AI systems look for "consensus signals" - agreement across multiple independent sources (your site + Reddit + G2 + YouTube + publications). G2 is the most-cited software-review platform: per Profound (Trevor Pyle, Head of Product Marketing), "For ChatGPT and Google AI Overviews, about 33% of reviews cited come from G2 - and for Perplexity, that percentage is closer to 75%," while Radix's 10,000+ search analysis put G2 first for software queries at 22.4% share of voice. For Sanddock this means: get listed/reviewed on G2 and Capterra, build authentic Reddit presence, maintain consistent brand/entity info, display author credentials and "Last updated" dates, and frame everything as educational/not financial advice. AI referral traffic also tends to convert better, though it is vertical-dependent: Seer Interactive's case study (Nick Haigler, R&D Lead) found ChatGPT referrals converting at 15.9%, Perplexity 10.5%, Claude 5.0%, Gemini 3.0%, vs Google Organic 1.76% - but Amsive's 54-site analysis found no significant overall difference (p=0.794), so treat the conversion premium as promising but not guaranteed.

**Technical hygiene:** Allow AI crawler bots in robots.txt (GPTBot, OAI-SearchBot, ChatGPT-User, ClaudeBot, Claude-SearchBot, PerplexityBot, Google-Extended). llms.txt is a proposed convention with no confirmed production use by major providers as of early 2026 - treat as cheap forward-looking hygiene, not a tactic to rely on.

### 3. Competitor content gap analysis

**What competitors all cover well (table-stakes - Sanddock must match then exceed):** technical-analysis basics (support/resistance, RSI, MACD, candlestick patterns), bot setup tutorials (grid, DCA), "best crypto signals Telegram" listicles, stop-loss/take-profit explainers, and platform-specific how-tos. Cryptohopper, Pionex, TradingView, and Altrady all run deep educational blogs; Pionex even publishes Reddit-question-format posts and "answered with real data" content (a GEO-smart format worth emulating).

**The gaps Sanddock can own:**
- **Transparency / "honest track record" content.** Competitors are widely criticized for deleting losing trades and cherry-picking wins. Independent tracking shows large gaps between *claimed* and *actual* win rates - SmartOptions.io's March 2026 report on Binance Killers: "executed 22 trades, achieving 14 wins and 4 losses, with 4 cancelled trades. This results in a 77.78% win rate" - versus its ~92% self-reported marketing claim. Sanddock's public, immutable, timestamped record showing wins AND losses is a content category competitors structurally cannot credibly produce.
- **Beginner-first, anti-hype answers to skeptical questions** - "are crypto signals worth it / legit," "do signal services delete losing trades," "what win rate is realistic." Mudrex openly states most retail traders lose money and signals don't reliably change that; the honest framing builds trust and is highly citable.
- **Heikin Ashi swing-detection methodology** - almost no signal competitor explains its actual signal logic transparently. Sanddock's HA swing-detection + AI-confidence-scoring explainer content is differentiated and educational.
- **Complaint-driven content opportunities** (from Trustpilot/Reddit/G2):
  - *3Commas:* pricing restructure backlash (legacy $50/mo plans replaced by higher tiers; "total scam" Trustpilot reviews), AI-first support frustration, fee-reporting inaccuracy, 2022 API security breach, no downgrades, and undisclosed AI Assistant token limits - per a 3Commas Trustpilot reviewer (publicly responded to by 3Commas on Jan 8, 2026), the AI Assistant is "limited to 1 million tokens per month across all paid plans...only about 500–1000 queries per month," a limit not clearly disclosed. Target: "3Commas hidden costs," "3Commas pricing explained," "3Commas alternative for beginners."
  - *Cornix:* auto-renewal without consent ("re-enabled it without my consent"), refusal of refunds, signal-provider fee-stacking, slow trade execution. Target: "Cornix auto-renewal," "Cornix cancellation," "Cornix vs [X]."
  - *Cryptohopper:* difficulty canceling subscriptions, unexpected charges, phishing/impersonation scam calls (their own banner warns "Cryptohopper will never call you"), bot malfunctions, expensive Hero plan ($129/mo). Target: "is Cryptohopper legit," "Cryptohopper cancellation," "Cryptohopper hidden fees."
  - *TrendSpider:* steep learning curve and price (Standard ~$54-89/mo, Premium ~$149/mo; 25 free AI messages/month run out fast). Target: "TrendSpider too expensive," "TrendSpider alternative for beginners."

**Are competitors already winning AI citations?** Several content-heavy players (altFINS, Pionex, Cryptohopper, review aggregators like SmartOptions, Mudrex, NFTEvening) already appear in AI-style "best crypto signals 2026" answers, largely because they publish transparent, frequently-updated, listicle-and-FAQ-structured content. None has locked up the "honest/transparent signal tool" entity - that position is open for Sanddock to claim through consistent multi-source presence.

### 4. Content format & structure recommendations

**Universal template (every Sanddock article):**
1. **H1** containing the exact target keyword + year where commercial ("Best AI Crypto Signals 2026").
2. **Answer-first TL;DR / direct-answer block** - 40-60 words immediately under H1 that fully answers the primary question (this does ~44% of citation work).
3. **Question-style H2s**, each followed immediately by a 40-60 word direct answer, then expansion.
4. **At least one comparison table** (for versus/alternative pages) and **numbered/ranked lists** for "best" content.
5. **Cited statistics with named sources** and verbatim phrasing (e.g., "independently tracked at 77.78%, per SmartOptions, March 2026").
6. **FAQ section** at the bottom targeting real People-Also-Ask / AI-style questions, marked up with FAQPage schema.
7. **Visible author byline + credentials + "Last updated [Month 2026]" date.**
8. **Schema:** Article + FAQPage on every post; HowTo on tutorials; SoftwareApplication + Organization on product/comparison pages.
9. **Mandatory risk/"not financial advice" disclaimer** (YMYL trust signal, on-brand for anti-hype voice).

**Word count by content type (match intent, don't pad):**
- Definition/quick-answer ("what is take profit"): 800-1,200 words; lead with a crisp definition.
- How-to ("how to read Heikin Ashi candles"): 1,200-1,800 words with numbered steps + screenshots.
- Comprehensive pillar guide ("Heikin Ashi strategy," "AI crypto signals explained"): 1,800-2,500 words.
- Comparison / "X alternative" / review: 1,500-2,200 words, table near the top, verdict + FAQ.
- "Best X 2026" listicle: 2,000-2,500 words, ranked Top-N, each entry in a consistent sub-template (who it's for, pricing, pros/cons).
Benchmark within ±20% of the top 3-5 ranking competitors per keyword; exceed only by adding genuine value (original track-record data, a unique FAQ), never filler.

**Prose vs lists for LLM extraction:** Use **both strategically** - a short answer-first prose paragraph (extractable as a quote) followed by a numbered/bulleted list (extractable as structured steps). Ranked numbered lists are the single best-citing format for commercial queries; concise question→answer prose pairs win informational citations.

**How to present "citable" statistics:** State the stat, the number, the named source, and the date in one sentence ("Sanddock's public track record shows an X% win rate across N signals since [date]"). Self-contained, sourced, dated sentences are what LLMs lift verbatim. Sanddock's own immutable track record is a proprietary-data asset - original data is the highest-leverage citation content type across all platforms.

## Recommendations

**Stage 1 (months 0-2) - Foundation + quick wins.** Ship the universal template + schema across the site; allow AI crawler bots; publish the low-difficulty, high-fit cluster first: "heikin ashi vs candlestick," "how to read heikin ashi candles," "what is take profit/stop loss," "are crypto signals legit," "how do crypto trading bots work." Build the public track-record hub as a flagship, data-rich, frequently-updated page. *Benchmark to advance: 10-15 pages indexed, first AI Overview/Perplexity citations appearing (test monthly by querying ChatGPT/Perplexity/Gemini with 15-20 buyer questions).*

**Stage 2 (months 2-5) - Commercial capture.** Publish competitor comparison/alternative/review pages (3Commas, Cryptohopper, Cornix, TrendSpider, WunderTrading, Altrady, Zignaly first - highest complaint volume = highest differentiation). Each must include an honest comparison table, the transparency angle, and an FAQ targeting "is X legit / hidden fees / cancellation." Simultaneously build off-site consensus: G2 + Capterra listings, authentic Reddit participation, YouTube explainers. *Benchmark: ranking page 1 for ≥5 "X review/alternative" terms; brand appearing in AI answers to "honest/transparent crypto signal tool."*

**Stage 3 (months 5-9) - Authority + head terms.** Attack the growing AI cluster ("AI crypto signals," "best AI crypto trading tools 2026," "agentic trading") and the harder head terms ("crypto trading signals," "crypto signals telegram") with comprehensive pillar guides interlinked to Stage 1/2 supporting content. Refresh every commercial page quarterly with current "2026/2027" signals and updated track-record stats. *Benchmark: top-3 for a head term; sustained AI citation share vs named competitors (track with Profound/Semrush/Ahrefs Brand Radar).*

**Thresholds that change the plan:** If after 90 days pages are indexed but not cited, the problem is structure (move answers higher, tighten to 40-60 word blocks, add stats/sources) - not length. If Perplexity referral traffic underperforms ChatGPT, increase freshness cadence and Reddit presence. If a competitor's complaint category gets resolved (e.g., 3Commas fixes pricing transparency), pivot that comparison page's angle to the next-weakest rival.

## Caveats
- **Search volumes are estimates, not measured facts.** Exact figures are gated behind paid Ahrefs/Semrush; the ranges here blend the few public numbers (clicks.so, thekollab) with informed estimates. Verify in a paid seat before allocating budget. Crypto volumes also swing hard with market cycles.
- **AI keyword demand is invisible to traditional tools.** Queries typed into ChatGPT/Perplexity are not in Keyword Planner/Ahrefs/Semrush data, so true demand for these topics is likely higher than the numbers suggest. Semrush's Prompt Research and similar tools are only beginning to measure AI search volume separately.
- **GEO metrics and best practices are fast-moving and partly correlational.** Many cited figures (citation lift %, schema correlations) come from vendor studies, not peer-reviewed sources - the notable exception being the Princeton/KDD 2024 GEO paper - and platform behavior changes frequently (ChatGPT model updates in 2026 changed citation counts). Re-audit quarterly.
- **YMYL/compliance risk.** Crypto signals are financial-adjacent. Maintain the anti-hype, educational, "not financial advice" framing rigorously - it is both a legal safeguard and, per the research, a genuine E-E-A-T/trust advantage. Never publish unverifiable win-rate claims; the public track record's credibility depends on never being cherry-picked.
- **Some competitor specifics (exact pricing, plan limits) change frequently** and should be re-verified at publication time, since out-of-date competitor data undermines the trust the strategy is built on.
