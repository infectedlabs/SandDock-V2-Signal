# Cryptohopper Review 2026 — Pricing, Pros, Cons & a Simpler Alternative

Cryptohopper is a cloud-based crypto trading bot platform that's been operating since 2017, with a free tier for manual trading and paid tiers ranging from roughly $29 to $129 per month for bot automation, strategy marketplace access, and signal integrations. It's a genuinely capable platform with a large user base, but it also has a few recurring friction points worth knowing before you subscribe — particularly around plan packaging and cancellation.

## What Cryptohopper offers

Cryptohopper's core product is automated bot trading — strategies you configure (or copy from its marketplace) that execute automatically based on technical indicators, signals, or copy-trading from other users. The free "Pioneer" tier allows manual trading without bot automation; paid tiers unlock increasing levels of bot automation, backtesting, and the strategy marketplace.

## The "15 trading bots" packaging confusion

One specific point of confusion that shows up repeatedly in user reviews is how Cryptohopper markets bot capacity. Marketing language referencing "15 trading bots" has been interpreted by some new users as 15 separate bot strategies, when the actual mechanism is closer to one bot configuration trading across up to 15 coins simultaneously. This distinction matters for setting expectations correctly before subscribing, and it's worth confirming directly on Cryptohopper's current plan page exactly what's included at your intended tier, since the terminology hasn't always been intuitive for new users.

## Common complaints worth knowing about

User reviews for Cryptohopper, like most platforms in this category, are mixed — a meaningful share of reviews are highly positive, while a separate cluster of negative reviews recurringly mentions difficulty cancelling subscriptions, unexpected charges after a trial period, and bot performance not matching backtested expectations in live market conditions. Cryptohopper has also publicly warned users about phishing attempts where scammers impersonate Cryptohopper support by phone — a reminder that the platform itself does not initiate support calls, and any such call should be treated as fraudulent regardless of how convincing it sounds.

## Pricing overview

Cryptohopper's paid tiers generally run from an entry-level plan in the $20–30/month range up to a top "Hero" tier around $129/month, with bot count, backtesting depth, and marketplace access scaling with each tier. As with most platforms in this space, confirm current pricing directly before subscribing, since tiers and inclusions are periodically adjusted.

## When Cryptohopper makes sense

If your goal is genuinely automated trading — strategies that execute without your manual involvement, copy-trading from other users, or backtesting complex configurations — Cryptohopper's depth is a real advantage, and the platform has years of iteration behind its automation features.

## When a simpler signal tool makes more sense

If what you actually want is to be told clearly when and why a trade setup has formed, with the decision to act left in your hands, a dedicated signal tool avoids the bot-configuration learning curve and the exchange API permissions that automation requires. ## Frequently asked questions

**Is Cryptohopper legit?**
Cryptohopper is an established, operating platform with a large user base, not a scam in the sense of being fraudulent by design. Like most platforms in this category, individual user experience varies, and it's worth reviewing current terms, cancellation policy, and realistic bot performance expectations before subscribing.

**Does Cryptohopper have a free trial?**
Cryptohopper offers a free tier (Pioneer) for manual trading; bot automation features require a paid plan, and free trials of paid tiers have varied over time — check current offers directly.

**Why do some users say Cryptohopper's bots underperformed expectations?**
This is a common pattern across bot-automation platforms generally — backtested results don't always translate directly to live performance, since live markets include slippage, fee impact, and conditions that didn't exist in the historical data used for backtesting. This isn't unique to Cryptohopper.

**Is a signal tool a good alternative to a bot platform like Cryptohopper?**
It depends on what you want. A signal tool is a good fit if you want to make the final trading decision yourself with a clear, explained alert. It's not a direct substitute if your specific goal is hands-off, automated execution.
