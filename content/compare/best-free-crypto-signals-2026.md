## What are the risks of using free Telegram signals?

The primary risks of using free Telegram signals include falling victim to pump-and-dump coordination, entering trades with hidden delay (slippage), registering on unregulated exchanges via referral traps, and executing trades lacking stop-losses, which leads to capital liquidation.

Most free Telegram channels are not charity projects; they are commercial operations. The common traps include:

1. **The Affiliate Trap**: The channel provides "free" signals, but only if you sign up for a specific, high-leverage exchange using their referral link. The admins then generate signals designed to keep you trading as frequently as possible, as they earn a commission on your transaction volume whether you win or lose.
2. **The Liquidity Exit**: Admins accumulate a small altcoin, post a "buy alert" to 50,000 free subscribers, and sell their positions into the massive buy-order rush.
3. **Signal Delaying**: The admin will post a signal in the VIP group first. If the price moves up, they post the signal to the free group hours later, claiming a "win," while the free users buy at the absolute top of the movement.

## How do you verify the accuracy of a free signal provider?

You verify the accuracy of a free signal provider by tracking their recommended trades in a spreadsheet for at least 30 days without trading, looking for deleted historical posts, checking if they calculate stop-losses realistically, and evaluating their net risk-to-reward ratio.

To conduct a manual audit, follow this framework:
* **Log Everything**: Note the exact timestamp, asset, entry price, stop-loss, and take-profit targets immediately when the signal is posted.
* **Watch for Deletions**: Take screenshots of the channel daily. At the end of the month, compare your log against the channel's visible history to see if they deleted losing trades.
* **Evaluate Risk-to-Reward (R:R)**: If a provider has an 80% win rate but their average win is 1% while their average loss is 10%, they are net-unprofitable. Ensure their average reward is at least 1.5 to 2 times larger than their average risk.

## How can you safely use free crypto signals to build a portfolio?

You can safely use free crypto signals by treating them as initial trade ideas rather than absolute commands, verifying each alert on your own charting software, executing trades manually with strict position sizing (risking maximum 1% per trade), and using paper trading to test new sources.

To build a safe workflow:

1. **Step 1: The Alert**: You receive a free alert (e.g., an RSI divergence alert from Sanddock).
2. **Step 2: Verification**: You open your own charting tool. You check if the asset has strong support levels nearby and ensure there is no major news event (like an SEC ruling or token unlock) occurring that day.
3. **Step 3: Risk Calculation**: You calculate your position size. If your account is $5,000 and you risk 1%, your maximum loss must be $50. If the stop-loss is 5% below entry, your trade size should be $1,000.
4. **Step 4: Execution**: You place the limit buy order, the stop-loss order, and the take-profit order simultaneously on your exchange. Never enter a trade without an active stop-loss order already on the exchange book.
