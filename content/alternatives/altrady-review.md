## What is Altrady and what features does it offer?

Altrady is an all-in-one cryptocurrency trading terminal that integrates with multiple exchanges via API. Key features include advanced charting, unified portfolio tracking, custom alerts, automated position sizes, and specialized order types.

Unlike traditional exchange interfaces, Altrady allows traders to manage their entire portfolio across platforms like Binance, OKX, Bybit, and Kraken from a single window. Features such as "Smart Orders" let users define entry, take-profit, and stop-loss rules before placing a trade, giving them professional-grade risk management. It also offers a "Crypto Base Scanner" that monitors markets for support and resistance levels, alerting traders when an asset reaches key price thresholds.

For traders who manage assets across multiple exchanges and want a unified, advanced dashboard to execute manual trades, Altrady offers a very robust set of tools.

## How does Altrady handle trade transparency and track record?

Altrady does not maintain a public strategy track record because it is a trading terminal, not a signal generator. It provides the execution tools, meaning trade performance is entirely dependent on the individual user's decisions.

When evaluating Altrady, it is important to realize that the platform does not recommend specific trades or guarantee a win rate. It acts strictly as an interface layer. If you use their scanners or custom alerts, you are the one configuring the parameters. Because of this, there is no public performance history or "win rate" to display. 

The success or failure of your trading on Altrady depends on your personal analysis and execution strategy. The platform gives you the cockpit, but you are the pilot.

## Why does signal transparency matter in crypto trading?

Signal transparency matters because without an unedited, public log of historical alerts, it is impossible to evaluate a strategy's real performance. Many services hide losing trades, making verifiable public logs crucial for building long-term trust.

In the cryptocurrency industry, many platforms advertise high returns and perfect win rates, only to delete or hide losing calls after the fact. This lack of transparency makes it extremely difficult for retail traders to separate genuine research from marketing hype. A transparent platform must:
- **Log every trade**: Show every signal generated, including entries, exits, and missed targets.
- **Maintain unedited logs**: Prevent any retrospect modification of historical signals.
- **Explain the rationale**: Provide clear technical reasons for why a trade was initiated, allowing users to learn the strategy rather than copying it blindly.

Without these standards, choosing a signal provider is a high-risk gamble.
