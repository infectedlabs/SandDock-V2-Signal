## What is WunderTrading and how does it work?

WunderTrading is an automation and copy-trading platform designed to help users bridge external trading signals with live cryptocurrency exchanges. The platform supports automated bot configuration, social copy-trading, and portfolio tracking across multiple accounts.

The core infrastructure of WunderTrading revolves around API integration. Users connect their exchange accounts (such as Bybit, Binance, Coinbase, or Kraken) using API keys. Once connected, WunderTrading acts as a central command dashboard. You can configure grid trading strategies, set up dollar-cost averaging (DCA) parameters, or browse a marketplace of third-party traders to copy their actions automatically.

Rather than conducting market analysis in-house, WunderTrading is built to execute strategies that you configure or subscribe to. It is an infrastructure provider rather than a signal provider.

## What is the WunderTrading TradingView bridge?

The WunderTrading TradingView bridge is a webhook integration that listens for alert alerts generated by TradingView strategies and executes them on connected exchange accounts. It requires users to write or buy custom Pine Script strategies.

Here is how the TradingView bridge workflow operates:
1. **Strategy Creation**: A user writes or imports a custom trading strategy in TradingView using Pine Script.
2. **Alert Setup**: The user configures an alert on TradingView to fire when specific technical indicators cross or meet set conditions.
3. **Webhook Bridge**: The alert triggers a webhook URL provided by WunderTrading, sending a JSON payload containing order details (e.g., asset, action, size).
4. **Execution**: WunderTrading receives the payload and instantly routes the trade command to the user's connected exchanges via API.

While highly powerful for algorithmic traders, this workflow relies entirely on the user's ability to create, test, and maintain a profitable TradingView strategy. It does not provide any market analysis or signal generation out of the box.

## The difference between an execution bridge and a signal generator

An execution bridge requires you to supply the trading strategies and signals, executing them automatically via API, while a signal generator produces the trading setups itself. WunderTrading is an execution bridge, whereas Sanddock is a signal generator.

Understanding this difference is critical for choosing the right tool:
- **WunderTrading (Execution Bridge)**: You are responsible for identifying *what* to trade and *when*. The platform handles the *how*-taking your rules and routing them to the exchange. If your strategy is flawed, WunderTrading will automate your losses.
- **Sanddock (Signal Generator)**: We run proprietary mathematical and AI models to identify high-probability setups (such as Heikin Ashi trend reversals). We provide you with the exact entry and exit coordinates, along with a transparent explanation of the setup. You do not need a TradingView subscription or coding knowledge to get actionable insights.

For traders who do not want to spend their time writing script indicators or debugging webhook payloads, a dedicated signal generator is a much simpler, lower-friction solution.

## WunderTrading pricing and plan limitations

WunderTrading's plans range from free to over $80 per month, with limits on active bots, open positions, and connected exchanges. Users must upgrade to higher tiers to run multiple automated strategies simultaneously, making it costly to scale.

WunderTrading structures its pricing across several tiers:
- **Free Tier**: Offers basic manual trading and limited bot execution, but restricts the user to a single exchange connection and low bot counts.
- **Starter Plan**: Typically priced around $10 to $15 per month, allowing a few active bots but restricting advanced order types like trailing stops.
- **Basic & Pro Plans**: Ranging from $30 to $50 per month, these plans unlock more concurrent positions and exchanges, though they still place limits on API throughput.
- **Premium Plan**: Approaching $80+ per month, this tier removes most restrictions, aimed at heavy algorithmic traders managing multiple accounts.

If you are a retail trader, these subscription fees add a layer of fixed overhead. If you also pay for a TradingView Pro subscription to get webhook alerts, your total monthly software costs can escalate quickly before you place a single trade.
